A.P.J. ABDUL KALAM

1931-2015

MISSILE MAN OF INDIA

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, often referred to as the "Missile Man of India," was a renowned Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development. Kalam was instrumental in the development of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and the Agni and Prithvi missiles. Known for his humility, visionary ideas, and dedication to education and youth, he authored several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "India 2020." Dr. Kalam's legacy as a scientist, educator, and leader continues to inspire millions across the globe.


As the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and the Secretary of the DRDO, he played a crucial role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, which established India as a nuclear power. Despite his scientific achievements, Kalam was deeply committed to social issues, particularly in the field of education. He believed that the youth of India were the country's greatest asset and tirelessly worked towards their empowerment. During his presidency, Dr. Kalam earned the nickname "People's President" for his approachable nature and efforts to connect with the common man. He remained a prolific writer and speaker, delivering numerous lectures and writing books that continue to motivate and guide future generations. Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, leaving behind a legacy of innovation, integrity, and inspiration. His life story continues to remind us of the power of dreams and the importance of perseverance and hard work.
